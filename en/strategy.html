🏛️ Capital & Turrets — Key Mechanics

The planner map reflects the real combat structure. The central hexagon is Capital territory — inside it shields cannot be used, making this zone an open battlefield.

🏰

Turret 1

Position: 6-7 on the clock. When occupied, automatically attacks the enemy Capital eliminating 1% of garrison soldiers at intervals.

🏰

Turret 2

Position: 10-11 on the clock. Same mechanic — key to draining enemy Capital garrison.

🏰

Turret 3

Position: 3 on the clock. Controlling all 3 turrets simultaneously is the winning strategy.

📋 Capital Combat Rules

  • Capital and Turrets allow a maximum of 10 garrison troops simultaneously
  • Within Capital range, HQ damage is doubled — beware of unshielded bases
  • When a Turret is occupied, it automatically attacks the enemy Capital, eliminating 1% of garrison soldiers
  • Field combat between survivors of the same server is not allowed
  • Attackers teleport for free with a 20-second cooldown — defenders must use the advanced teleport item

🎯 Strategies by Role

⚔️ Attacker Strategy

  • At 00:30 the strongest alliances teleport to the enemy server to destroy bases — weaker players wait
  • At 12:00 all forces focus on the 3 turrets — taking all 3 first automatically drains the Capital
  • Coordinate using the map clock — "we attack Turret 2 at 11, cover the 3"
  • Send support alliances to block defender reinforcements outside the hexagon
  • Once turrets are taken, focus everything on the Capital to reach 100%

🛡️ Defender Strategy

  • Days before Saturday: move alliance bases around the Capital to block space from the enemy
  • Place farm accounts with shields outside the hexagon to block access routes
  • Move Alliance Centers to occupy space and hinder enemy teleportation inside the territory
  • Keep the 10 garrison slots of the Capital and turrets filled with your best troops
  • Remember: inside the hexagon there are no shields — protect weaker bases by moving them outside

🕐 How to Use the Clock System

The planner clock replicates the time reference players use to coordinate attacks. Instead of "attack from the left", say "attack at 9" — everyone knows exactly where.
