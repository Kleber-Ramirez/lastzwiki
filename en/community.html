Last Z Community

Over 10 million active survivors worldwide. Here you'll find the resources you need to connect, learn and grow alongside the Last Z Survival Shooter community.

When I first started Last Z, I played completely alone — no alliance, no Discord, no idea that such a large community existed behind the game. I progressed much slower than I could have, and made mistakes that a single question on Discord would have saved me from. That experience is why I created Last Z Wiki.

The Last Z English-speaking community is large and active. There are players on Discord and Facebook who have been playing for months or years and are genuinely willing to help. The biggest obstacle I see consistently is that new players don't know where to look for information or who to talk to. This page exists to change that.

Here I've gathered all the official resources, explain how to get value from Discord without getting lost in the chaos of general chat, and share what I've learned about connecting with other players who are in the same server as you.

🌟 Why connecting with the community makes a real difference

🧠

Access to up-to-date information

The Last Z meta changes every season. Active players on Discord or Facebook detect changes before any guide does — strategies that worked in S2 can be useless in S4. Staying connected with the community is the fastest way to keep up without having to discover everything on your own.

🤝

Finding an alliance and teammates

One of the most frequent questions I get is "how do I find a good English-speaking alliance in my server?". The answer is always the same: Discord. Alliance recruitment channels are where R5 leaders post vacancies and players look for groups. Without Discord, you're completely dependent on whatever shows up in your server.

💡

Getting answers in real time

Last Z has mechanics that aren't explained well in-game — formations, skill coefficients, resource priority, which events are worth skipping. Veteran players in Discord channels answer these questions in minutes. What took me weeks to understand through trial and error, you can know today with a single question.

📢

Patch notes and news before anyone else

The Last Z team announces updates, new heroes and meta changes first in official Discord and Facebook channels. Being subscribed means you're never surprised by a patch — you always know what's changing with enough time to adjust your strategy before the rest of your server catches on.

💬 How to get value from Last Z Discord without getting lost

The official Last Z Discord can be overwhelming at first — dozens of channels and hundreds of messages per hour in general chat. What I do is simple: I almost completely ignore general chat and focus on two or three specific channels. The announcements channel for official news, the alliance recruitment channel when I need to find players, and the FAQ channel when I have a tactical question. With that 5-minute daily routine you get 80% of the value without the noise.

🔔

Mute general chat: Enable notifications only for mentions and the announcements channel. Everything else generates too much noise and distracts without providing useful information for your daily progress.

🔍

Search before asking: 90% of questions about basic mechanics have already been answered in the FAQ channel. Searching first saves time and avoids repetitive responses from other players.

🌍

Find region-specific channels: The official server has channels by region. Look for the English or North America channel — you'll find players who are likely in servers close to yours.

📋

Note your state number: When asking for help or looking for an alliance, always mention your state number. Without that information nobody can help you connect with players from the same server.
